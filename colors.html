Colors

Atmosphere

For me, one of the most manual, but also rewarding color projects was a mural representing the seasons of the year. This was a great opportunity to explore in depth, how colors and certain color combinations impact mood and atmosphere.

Color hunting

This was a fun project where a great deal of time was spent searching through magazines for interesting colors to create the right color combinations. In fact, most of the manual effort was spent cutting each squares and most of the squares were not even used.

Color theory is fascinating

I got some amazing guidance from a book entitled "Modern Colors of Japan - The Complete Guide for Designers and Graphic Artists" by Teruko Sakurai. This helped me to validate some of the different color selections that I made. I had way too many colors to sort through so I also reviewed how to make origami holders.

Spring

The atmosphere is warming and the snow has melted. New plant growth emerges.

Summer

The sun is at the steepest angle, maximizing the antioxidants and pigmentation of the fruits and flowers.

This can be seen in making, not an equilateral, but an isosceles triangle on the color wheel.

Autumn

The leaves begin to change color as the amount of chlorophyll in them is reduced and new pigments of yellow, orange, red, purple and brown are seen.

Brown is quite a versatile color, matching with almost anything so this combination was easy to make without assistance of the color wheel.

Winter

Finally winter time with snow and ice. The shimmery effect was easy to create using cutouts from jewellery pages. Purple has been included, and resembles cold outer space.

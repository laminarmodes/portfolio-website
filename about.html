From Academia to Industry

Science & Engineering

I was born in the U.S.A, have family across multiple countries and continents and have been living and working in France and Singapore since 2009. I was educated in Electrical / Computer Engineering and Mathematics while studying Computational Electromagnetics and Fuid Dynamics. My favorite tools were Java, Matlab, R and various Multi-Physics Simulators. I performed research in Flexible & Printable Electronics then worked in Satcom, Sensors and Radar.

Decision Science

Data Storytelling, Design Thinking and UX/UI Design

I started working as a Decision Scientist at Rolls-Royce R2 Data Labs in 2017. This role is different to that of a Data Scientist in that it involves taking Data Science outputs and translating them into a story that the user can consume and extract value from. I actually worked the opposite direction, harnessing Design Thinking to first determine what value there is to bring and what story needs to be told, before plugging in the analytics. I then moved towards the traditional UX Process in order to carry the business cases all the way to tangible protypes that I could validate through formal usability testing.

I grew into a new role of UX Lead where I helped various digital teams perform user research, problem definition, ideation, design, prototyping and testing.

I am now a Product Owner & UX Lead, focusing on buildling a solution to quantify user experience across the business.

Bringing Experiences to Life

Deep Dive into Design & Development

I spend very a large amount of time using SwiftUI, Kotlin and Jetpack Compose to test new user interactions and experiences. These are three beautiful programming languages that have really solidified my confidence in user interface design.

I am excited about native design systems, particularly for displaying analytics data, and so I am spending a great deal of time learning the fundamentals of information visualization and graphic design in order to help me come up with new ways to tell stories with data.

Even more, I was am excited about the idea of SwiftUI paving the way for new Augmented Reality experiences. More on that, soon!
